2 This module, `kcp_ss_learner` (Kernel Change Point Sample Scorer Learner), includes a wrapper to
3 perform anomaly scoring as understood in the TADkit formalism, based on this library's change-point
4 detection methodology.
5  
6 The kernel change-point detection algorithm outputs a final list of time indices at which it thinks

8  
9 Other methods output a score at __all__ time indices, where the higher the score is, the more we

10 believe that "something happened there".
11  
12 In order to allow the kernel change-point method to be integrated into a Python package based
13 around score samples, we have implemented a function __kcp_ss__ which takes the __kcp_ds__ output
14 list of change-points and turns them into scores at __all time indices__.
15  
16 Remember that the kernel change-point detection algorithm truly believes that it has found the true
17 and only set of change-points. However, due to random noise, it could have been that a true
18 change-point was immediately before or immediately after a detected change-point, i.e., 1 or 2 or
19 maybe even 3 time indices before or after.
20  
21 We give a score of 1 at each detected change-point. Other points are assigned a score that
22 decreases as they move away from the detected change-point, in a way parameterized by a decay
23 parameter (gamma).
